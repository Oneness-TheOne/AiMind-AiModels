ABSTRACT
Establishing Interpretation System and Improving
Validity of HTP (House-Tree-Person) Test
Baek, Won Dae
Major in Military Counseling
Department of Counseling Psychology
Graduate School of Sahmyook University
This study is to observe the relationship between the response
characteristics of elementary school students by the level of personality
attributes and to confirm the usefulness of HTP (House-Tree-Person) test as
a projective testing tool that can distinguish psychological statements of
elementary school students through HTP test. Because various projective
tests basically used in the clinical site doesn't be standardized specifically, the
criteria for judgment and interpretation have a big gap depending on a
counselor's ability. To decline this gap, we need to escalate the filial of
projective test how we make systemic checking-program Purpose of this
study is to make the HTP test can be used usefully in counseling site by

establishing the interpretation system of HTP test that its usefulness gets
recognition not enough and verifying its validity. Especially, I will check
usefulness as a diagnostic testing tool that can distinguish the general
development of element school students, relationships between the response
characteristics by the level of personality attributes, and psychological
statements. And I will increase the utilization of the HTP test as a testing
tool for kids.
On study 1, the representations usually represented in clinical site were
sorted and rechecked how were compared with representations of a prior
study to verify existing the interpretative system and define new
representative system. And this study is aimed as a tool that identifies the
usefulness of the diagnostic inspection tool based on the image that can
distinguish general development, relationship of response characteristics by the
level of personality attributes, and psychological state, and diagnoses children,
especially elementary school kids.
On study 2, The subjects of this study were 343 people(boy: 173, girl: 170)
who are in 4th, 5th, 6th grade in Seoul and they tested self-reporting test
and HTP test. Collected data were verified by SPSS 23.0 through independent
sample t-testing, ANOVA, and analysis of optimal correlation of Pearson. The
main results of this research are as follows.
First, boys get higher figure than girls' in the tests on aggression, and
